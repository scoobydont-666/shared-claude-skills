# TCP Blueprint — Federal Tax (2026)

> **OBBBA UPDATE APPLIED 2026-03-23** — P.L. 119-21, signed July 4, 2025.
> TCJA provisions made permanent unless noted. Sources: IRS, Tax Foundation, Kitces, PKF O'Connor Davies.

Operate at Application 55–65% and Analysis 25–35% across all four TCP areas. This reference contains the complete technical knowledge base for federal tax compliance and planning.

---

## Area I — Individual Tax (~37% weight)

### Filing Status & Dependency
- Five filing statuses: Single, MFJ, MFS, HOH, QSS — determination rules and optimization
- HOH requirements: unmarried, >50% household costs, qualifying person (§2(b))
- Dependency exemptions eliminated by TCJA but dependency status still drives credits
- Qualifying child (§152(c)): relationship, age, residency, support, joint return tests
- Qualifying relative (§152(d)): relationship, gross income, support, not a qualifying child
- Tie-breaker rules for multiple claims (§152(c)(4))
- ITIN holders: filing obligations, credit eligibility limitations

### Gross Income (§61 and Beyond)
- §61 all-inclusive definition — wages, interest, dividends, business income, gains, rents, royalties, alimony (pre-2019 agreements), pensions, etc.
- Compensation: W-2 wages, tips, fringe benefits (§132 exclusions), group-term life (§79), employer-provided vehicles
- Investment income: qualified vs. ordinary dividends, OID, tax-exempt interest, Series I/EE bond interest exclusion (§135)
- Retirement distributions: traditional IRA/401(k) taxation, Roth ordering rules, 72(t) SEPP, RMD rules (SECURE 2.0 age changes)
- Social Security taxation: provisional income thresholds ($25K/$32K/$34K/$44K), up to 85% includable
- Cancellation of debt income (§108): insolvency exception, qualified principal residence exclusion, purchase price reduction
- Rental income: Schedule E reporting, vacation home rules (§280A), 14-day/$15K test
- Gambling income: session method, professional gambler vs. recreational, Form W-2G thresholds
- Digital assets / cryptocurrency: realization events, basis tracking methods (FIFO, specific ID), broker reporting (Form 1099-DA starting 2025+)

### Exclusions from Gross Income
- §101 life insurance proceeds (transfer-for-value exceptions)
- §102 gifts and inheritances
- §104 damages (physical injury vs. emotional distress)
- §105/§106 employer health plans
- §117 qualified scholarships
- §119 meals/lodging for employer convenience
- §121 principal residence gain exclusion ($250K/$500K), ownership + use tests, partial exclusion, divorce situations
- §129 dependent care assistance ($5,000 limit)
- §132 fringe benefits: no-additional-cost, qualified employee discount, working condition, de minimis, qualified transportation ($315/mo transit, $315/mo parking — check current inflation adjustment)
- §139 qualified disaster relief payments

### Above-the-Line Deductions (§62)
- Educator expenses ($300, indexed)
- Self-employment tax deduction (50% of SE tax)
- Self-employed health insurance deduction (§162(l))
- HSA contributions (§223): individual $4,300 / family $8,550 (2026 est. — verify), catch-up $1,000, HDHP requirements
- IRA deductions: traditional ($7,000/$8,000 catch-up), phase-outs by AGI and active participant status
- Student loan interest ($2,500 max, AGI phase-out)
- Alimony (pre-2019 instruments only)
- Moving expenses (military only post-TCJA)

### Itemized Deductions (Schedule A)
- Medical expenses: 7.5% AGI floor, qualifying expenses, timing strategies
- State and local taxes (SALT): $40,000 cap (OBBBA, 2025–2029; $20,000 MFS), increases 1%/yr through 2029. AGI phasedown: 30% × (MAGI − $500K threshold, also +1%/yr); floor = $10,000. Reverts to $10,000 in 2030. Income/sales tax election, real property tax, personal property tax.
- Mortgage interest: acquisition debt limit $750K permanent (OBBBA; $375K MFS), pre-12/15/2017 $1M grandfathered. Home equity interest: OBBBA codified tracing rules — deductible only if proceeds used to buy/build/improve secured home (permanent). Points deductible.
- Charitable contributions: cash (60% AGI), appreciated property (30% AGI), qualified conservation easements, substantiation requirements ($250+, $500+, $5K+), carryforward rules. **OBBBA (2026+):** (A) 0.5% AGI floor — first 0.5% of AGI in donations is non-deductible; (B) 37% bracket cap at 35% effective rate — reduction = lesser of (2/37 × allowable itemized deductions) or (2/37 × taxable income above 37% threshold). Pease limitation permanently repealed.
- Casualty losses: federally declared disaster only (post-TCJA), $100 floor per event, 10% AGI floor
- Standard deduction (permanent, OBBBA): 2025: $15,750 single / $31,500 MFJ; 2026: $16,100 single / $32,200 MFJ / $24,150 HOH. New OBBBA senior deduction (2025–2028 only): $6,000/person age 65+, phases out at 6%/dollar above $75K single / $150K MFJ (separate from existing additional standard deduction of $2,050 single/$1,650 MFJ for 65+). Personal exemptions permanently eliminated.

### Qualified Business Income Deduction (§199A)
- 20% deduction on QBI from pass-through entities and sole proprietorships — **made permanent by OBBBA**
- Specified service trade or business (SSTB) definitions: health, law, consulting, athletics, financial services, actuarial, performing arts, brokerage
- W-2 wage limitation: greater of (a) 50% of W-2 wages, or (b) 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of UBIA of qualified property
- Taxable income thresholds for phase-in of limitations: OBBBA raised to $75,000 single / $150,000 MFJ (2026+; was ~$50K/$100K). New minimum deduction: $400 for taxpayers with ≥$1,000 QBI from materially participated business (§199A(i), inflation-adjusted after 2026)
- Aggregation elections under Reg. §1.199A-4
- QBI loss carryforward mechanics
- REIT dividends and PTP income treatment
- **CRITICAL: California does NOT conform to §199A** — see california-tax.md

### Capital Gains & Losses
- Short-term vs. long-term: 12-month holding period rule, netting mechanics
- Preferential rates: 0%/15%/20% brackets + 3.8% NIIT
- §1(h) ordering rules: 28% rate (collectibles), 25% rate (unrecaptured §1250 gain)
- §1231 hotchpot: net gain = LTCG treatment, net loss = ordinary; 5-year lookback recapture
- §1245 recapture: ordinary income on gain to extent of depreciation taken on personal property
- §1250 recapture: ordinary income on excess depreciation (§1(h)(1)(E) unrecaptured at 25%)
- Capital loss limitations: $3,000/year ($1,500 MFS), unlimited carryforward
- Wash sale rules (§1091): 61-day window, basis adjustment to replacement shares
- Constructive sales (§1259)
- Installment method (§453): gross profit percentage, imputed interest, related party rules
- Net Investment Income Tax (§1411): 3.8% on lesser of NII or MAGI over $200K/$250K

### Tech Equity Taxation (Bay Area Specialty)
- **ISOs (§422)**: no regular tax at exercise, AMT preference for bargain element, qualifying disposition (1yr from exercise + 2yr from grant), disqualifying disposition = ordinary income
- **NSOs (§83)**: ordinary income at exercise = FMV minus exercise price, employer deduction mirrors inclusion, §83(b) election on restricted stock
- **RSUs**: taxed as ordinary income at vesting (no §83(b) election available for standard RSUs), employer withholding requirements
- **ESPP (§423)**: qualified plan = 15% discount, $25K annual limit, disposition rules mirror ISO logic
- **Section 83(i)**: qualified stock election for private company employees — 5-year deferral on RSU/option income
- **California AMT trap**: ISO exercise creates California AMT liability even when federal AMT credit offsets federal — devastating for tech employees exercising large grants

### Passive Activity Rules (§469)
- Material participation: 7 tests (500 hours, substantially all, >100 hours + most, significant participation aggregation, 5 of 10 years, personal service, facts & circumstances)
- Rental activities: per se passive (exception for real estate professionals — 750 hours + >50% test)
- $25,000 rental loss allowance: active participation, $100K–$150K AGI phase-out
- Grouping elections (Reg. §1.469-4): once made, generally irrevocable
- Passive activity disposition: fully suspended losses released on complete taxable disposition
- Self-charged interest and management fee recharacterization rules

### AMT (§55–§59)
- AMT made permanent by OBBBA. Key 2026 changes: phaseout thresholds reset and phaseout rate doubled.
- AMTI computation: start with taxable income, add back preferences and adjustments
- Key add-backs: SALT deduction, ISO bargain element, incentive stock option timing, tax-exempt interest on private activity bonds, percentage depletion excess
- Exemption amounts (2026): $90,100 single / $140,200 MFJ (permanent, indexed)
- Phase-out (2026): begins at $500,000 single / $1,000,000 MFJ. **Rate increased from 25% to 50%** — exemption lost $0.50 per $1 over threshold. More taxpayers hit AMT faster.
- Two-tier rate: 26% on first ~$239K ($119.5K MFS), 28% on excess (verify 2026 bracket)
- AMT credit (§53): used in subsequent years when regular tax exceeds tentative minimum tax

### Individual Credits
- Child Tax Credit (§24): $2,200 per qualifying child under 17 (OBBBA, up from $2,000, indexed after 2025), $500 ODC, AGI phase-out $200K/$400K. Refundable portion (ACTC): verify 2026 amount.
- Child & Dependent Care Credit (§21): 20–35% of up to $3,000/$6,000 expenses
- Education credits: AOTC (§25A(i)) up to $2,500, 40% refundable, 4-year limit; LLC (§25A(b)) up to $2,000, nonrefundable
- Earned Income Credit (§32): refundable, 0–3 qualifying children, investment income limit
- Retirement Savings Credit (§25B): nonrefundable, AGI limits by filing status
- Premium Tax Credit (§36B): marketplace coverage, FPL sliding scale
- Adoption Credit (§23): up to $16,810 (2024), special-needs automatic max
- Energy credits: residential clean energy (§25D) 30%, home efficiency (§25C) up to $3,200/year

### Self-Employment Tax (§1401)
- 15.3% on net SE income up to OASDI wage base ($168,600 for 2024 — verify) + 2.9% Medicare on all SE income
- Additional 0.9% Medicare tax on SE income over $200K/$250K (§1401(b)(2))
- 92.35% multiplier
- 50% above-the-line deduction
- SE tax impact on QBI computation (reduced by deductible portion)

### Estimated Payments & Penalties
- Quarterly due dates: 4/15, 6/15, 9/15, 1/15
- Safe harbors: 100% prior year tax (110% if AGI >$150K) or 90% current year
- Annualized income installment method (Form 2210 Schedule AI) — critical for tech employees with concentrated income events
- Underpayment penalty is per-quarter, no offset between quarters

## Area II — Entity Tax (~28% weight)

### C Corporations (§11, Subchapter C)
- Flat 21% federal rate (TCJA permanent)
- Taxable income computation: gross income minus deductions (§161–§199A N/A to C-corps)
- §291 preference cutback: 20% of excess of ACRS/MACRS deduction over SL on real property
- Dividends Received Deduction (§243): 50% (<20% ownership), 65% (20–79%), 100% (80%+ affiliated)
- NOL: unlimited carryforward, limited to 80% of taxable income (no carryback except farming/insurance)
- Accumulated Earnings Tax (§531): 20% on accumulated taxable income exceeding reasonable needs ($250K/$150K personal service)
- Personal Holding Company Tax (§541): 20% on undistributed PHC income (passive income >60% of AOGI)
- Built-in gains on conversions from C to S (§1374)
- Charitable contribution: 10% of taxable income (computed before DRD, NOL deduction, charitable deduction), 5-year carryforward
- Capital loss: deductible only against capital gains, 3-year carryback / 5-year carryforward
- §351 transfers: control requirement (80%), boot recognition, basis rules
- Distributions: §301 (dividend to extent of E&P), §302 (redemption treated as exchange), §303 (death tax redemption), §304 (related corp acquisitions)
- Liquidation: §331 (shareholder gain/loss), §336 (corporate gain/loss recognition), §332/§337 (subsidiary liquidation nonrecognition)
- Estimated tax: required if tax ≥$500, large corporation rule (prior year safe harbor only for Q1)

### S Corporations (§1361–§1379)
- Eligibility: domestic corp, ≤100 shareholders (family aggregation), only individuals/estates/certain trusts/exempt orgs, one class of stock, no NRA shareholders
- Election: Form 2553, unanimous consent, effective date rules (2½ month rule)
- Revocation: majority of shares, prospective or retroactive
- Termination: cease to qualify, passive investment income + C-corp E&P for 3 consecutive years
- Income/loss flow-through: pro-rata per-share-per-day allocation, election to close books (§1377(a)(2))
- Basis tracking (critical):
  - Stock basis: initial investment + income allocations + additional contributions – distributions – loss allocations – nondeductible expenses
  - Debt basis: direct loans from shareholder to corp only (no guarantees), separately tracked
  - Loss limitation ordering: stock basis first, then debt basis (§1366(d))
  - At-risk rules (§465): applied after basis limitation
  - Passive activity rules (§469): applied after at-risk
- AAA tracking: accumulated adjustments account, ordering rules for distributions
- E&P from prior C-corp years: affects distribution characterization after AAA exhausted
- Built-in gains tax (§1374): 21% on net recognized built-in gain during recognition period (5 years post-conversion)
- LIFO recapture (§1363(d)): owed in final C-corp year if converting from C

### Partnerships (§701–§777, Subchapter K)
- Formation (§721): generally nonrecognition, exceptions for services, disguised sales (§707(a)(2)(B)), investment company
- Partner's basis: outside basis = contributions + income allocations + liabilities – distributions – losses
- §704(b) substantial economic effect: allocations must have economic effect or be in accordance with partner's interest
- §704(c) contributed property: built-in gain/loss allocated to contributing partner (traditional, curative, remedial methods)
- §704(d) loss limitation: limited to outside basis
- Guaranteed payments (§707(c)): determined without regard to partnership income, ordinary income to partner, deductible by partnership
- Distributions:
  - Current (§731): no gain unless cash > basis, basis reduction
  - Liquidating (§731/§732): different basis rules, gain/loss recognition on cash/unrealized receivables/inventory only
  - §751(b) disproportionate distributions: hot asset exchange treatment
- Sale of partnership interest (§741): capital gain/loss, except §751 hot assets (ordinary income portion)
  - Hot assets: unrealized receivables (including §1245/§1250 recapture) + inventory items
  - §743(b) basis adjustment: available if §754 election in effect or substantial built-in loss
- §754 election: permanent, applies to all future transfers and distributions (§743(b) and §734(b))
- Partnership liabilities (§752): recourse vs. nonrecourse allocation rules, deficit restoration obligations
- Technical termination: eliminated by TCJA (no more §708(b)(1)(B))
- Guaranteed payments to retired partners (§736): §736(a) payments (ordinary income) vs. §736(b) (property payments)
- Family partnerships (§704(e)): gift of capital interest, reasonable compensation for donor-partner

### Tax-Exempt Organizations
- §501(c)(3) qualification: organized and operated exclusively for exempt purposes, no private inurement, no substantial lobbying, no campaign intervention
- Form 990 filing: revenue thresholds, Schedule requirements (A, B, C, D, etc.)
- UBTI (§511–§514): trade or business, regularly carried on, not substantially related
- UBTI exceptions: dividends, interest, rents (unless debt-financed), royalties, capital gains
- Debt-financed income (§514): taxable percentage based on average acquisition indebtedness
- Excess compensation excise tax (§4960): 21% on compensation >$1M to covered employees
- Excess business holdings (§4943): private foundations
- Specific deduction: $1,000 against UBTI (except for controlling organizations)

### Trusts & Estates (Subchapter J)
- Simple vs. complex trust distinction
- Distributable Net Income (DNI, §643): ceiling on deduction for distributions, character flow-through
- Distribution deduction (§651/§661): tier system for complex trusts
- §663(b) 65-day election: treat early distributions as prior year
- Compressed brackets: 37% rate kicks in at ~$14,450 (2024 — verify)
- Grantor trust rules (§671–§679): reversionary interest, power to control beneficial enjoyment, administrative powers, revocable trusts
- Qualified Subchapter S Trust (QSST) and Electing Small Business Trust (ESBT) rules
- Estimated tax: same rules as individuals (no prior year safe harbor for estates in first 2 years)

## Area III — Property Transactions (~12% weight)

### Basis Determination
- Cost basis (§1012): purchase price + acquisition costs
- Gifted property (§1015): donor's basis (gain), FMV if lower (loss), split basis rule
- Inherited property (§1014): FMV at date of death (or alternate valuation date §2032), community property full step-up for both halves
- Converted property: lower of adjusted basis or FMV at conversion date (personal to business)
- Stock basis: specific identification, FIFO default, average cost (mutual funds)

### Cost Recovery
- MACRS (§168): recovery periods (3, 5, 7, 10, 15, 20, 27.5, 39 year), conventions (half-year, mid-quarter, mid-month)
- §179 expensing: $1,220,000 limit (2024 — verify), $3,050,000 phase-out, qualifying property, SUV limitation ($28,900)
- Bonus depreciation (§168(k)): 100% for assets placed in service before 1/1/2023, phasing down 20% per year (2023: 80%, 2024: 60%, 2025: 40%, 2026: 20%, 2027: 0%) — **California decouples, see california-tax.md**
- Listed property (§280F): >50% business use requirement, luxury auto limits
- Amortization: §197 intangibles (15-year), start-up costs (§195, $5,000 + amortize remainder), organizational costs (§248/§709)

### Like-Kind Exchanges (§1031)
- Real property only (post-TCJA), held for productive use in trade/business or investment
- Boot recognition: gain recognized to extent of boot received
- Basis in replacement: basis of relinquished – boot received + gain recognized + boot given
- Qualified intermediary requirements, 45-day identification / 180-day completion deadlines
- Reverse exchange rules (Rev. Proc. 2000-37)
- Related party restrictions (§1031(f)): 2-year holding requirement
- **California conformity**: generally conforms but clawback risk on out-of-state moves

### Other Property Transactions
- Involuntary conversions (§1033): 2-year replacement (3 years for condemned real property), similar or related in service/use
- Installment sales (§453): gross profit percentage, imputed interest (§483/§7872), related party resale within 2 years (§453(e))
- Related party losses (§267): disallowed between related parties, constructive ownership rules
- Wash sales (§1091): 61-day window (30 before, day of, 30 after), basis transferred to replacement
- Opportunity Zones (§1400Z-2): invest capital gains within 180 days, defer gain until 2026 or earlier disposition, 10-year hold for basis step-up. OBBBA did not extend or materially modify OZ provisions — verify current status for any subsequent legislation. **California decoupled entirely.**

## Area IV — Tax Compliance & Planning (~23% weight)

### Filing & Procedural
- Individual due dates: 4/15 (extension to 10/15), fiscal year rules
- Entity due dates: partnerships/S-corps 3/15, C-corps 4/15, estates/trusts 4/15 (calendar year)
- Extension rules: automatic 6-month (Form 4868 individual, 7004 entity), payment still due by original date
- Statute of limitations: 3 years general (§6501(a)), 6 years if >25% gross income omission (§6501(e)), unlimited if fraud or no return (§6501(c))
- Claim for refund: 3 years from filing or 2 years from payment, whichever is later (§6511)
- Amended returns: Form 1040-X, Form 1120-X, superseding returns

### Penalties & Interest
- Failure to file (§6651(a)(1)): 5%/month up to 25%, minimum penalty for returns >60 days late
- Failure to pay (§6651(a)(2)): 0.5%/month up to 25%
- Accuracy-related (§6662): 20% on substantial understatement ($5,000 individual), negligence, valuation misstatement
- Fraud (§6663): 75% of underpayment attributable to fraud
- Information return penalties (§6721/§6722): tiered by correction date
- Reasonable cause defense: §6664(c)(1), Reg. §1.6664-4, good-faith reliance on professional
- First-time abatement: administrative waiver for taxpayers with clean 3-year history

### IRS Examination & Appeals
- DIF score selection, classification, assignment
- Correspondence audit vs. office audit vs. field audit
- IDR (Information Document Request) response strategy
- 30-day letter: right to appeal
- Appeals conference: settlement authority, hazards of litigation
- 90-day letter (statutory notice of deficiency): Tax Court petition deadline
- Tax Court: regular procedure vs. small case ($50K), burden of proof (§7491)
- Collection: CP501/CP504 notices, federal tax lien (§6321), levy (§6331)
- CDP hearing (§6320/§6330): 30-day request window, Tax Court review
- Installment agreements (§6159): guaranteed ($10K), streamlined ($50K), partial payment
- Offers in Compromise (§7122): doubt as to liability, doubt as to collectibility, effective tax administration
- Innocent spouse relief (§6015): traditional, separate liability, equitable relief

### Tax Planning Strategies
- Income timing: deferral (installment sales, deferred compensation, opportunity zones), acceleration (when rates expected to rise)
- Deduction timing: prepayment strategies, bunching itemized deductions, QCD strategies
- Income shifting: family entity structures, kiddie tax (§1(g)) limitations
- Entity selection: sole prop vs. LLC vs. S-corp vs. C-corp — effective tax rate modeling
- Roth conversion analysis: current bracket vs. projected future bracket, fill-up-the-bracket strategy, Medicare IRMAA impact
- Retirement planning: contribution strategies, employer match optimization, gpu-node-3 backdoor Roth
- Charitable planning: donor-advised funds, CRTs, CLTs, bunching strategy, QCDs (§408(d)(8))
- Estate/gift integration: annual exclusion ($19,000/2026), lifetime exemption $15M/person permanent (OBBBA, indexed starting 2027). $30M per couple. GST exemption matches. Far fewer estates subject to federal estate tax.
- **OBBBA new planning items**: tip/overtime income exclusions (2025–2028, verify scope), senior standard deduction ($6,000/person through 2028), SALT cap $40K with AGI phasedown, 0.5% AGI charitable floor + 37% bracket itemized deduction cap (permanent), Roth catch-up requirement for high earners ($145K+), 100% bonus depreciation restored permanently, employer childcare credit increase.
- Year-end planning checklist: harvest losses, harvest gains in 0% bracket, Roth conversions, charitable giving, estimated payment true-up, §179/bonus depreciation timing
