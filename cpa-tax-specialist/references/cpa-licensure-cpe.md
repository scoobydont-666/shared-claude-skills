## 1. CPA Exam Structure (2024 Evolution Model)

The Uniform CPA Examination transitioned to the new Evolution model effective January 2024.

### Core Sections (All Candidates Must Pass)
- **AUD** — Auditing and Attestation (4 hours)
- **FAR** — Financial Accounting and Reporting (4 hours)
- **REG** — Taxation and Regulation (4 hours)

### Discipline Sections (Choose One)
- **BAR** — Business Analysis and Reporting
- **ISC** — Information Systems and Controls
- **TCP** — Tax Compliance and Planning ← Your specialty

### TCP Discipline Section Detail
- 4-hour exam, task-based simulations + MCQs
- Content areas:
  - Area I: Individual Tax (~37%)
  - Area II: Entity Tax (~28%)
  - Area III: Property Transactions (~12%)
  - Area IV: Tax Compliance & Planning (~23%)
- Skill allocation: Application 55–65%, Analysis 25–35%
- Passing score: 75 on 0–99 scale
- 30-month rolling window to pass all four sections

### Exam Administration
- Administered by NASBA/AICPA via Prometric
- Continuous testing year-round (no more testing windows as of 2024)
- Score release: typically rolling, check NASBA timeline
- International testing available at select locations

## 2. California Licensure — Legacy/Current Path

This path remains fully valid and will be accepted through the transition period ending December 31, 2028.

### Education Requirements
- **Baccalaureate degree** from an accredited institution
- **150 semester units** total (225 quarter units)
- Specific subject requirements:
  - **24 semester units in accounting subjects**: financial accounting, auditing, cost/managerial accounting, taxation, accounting information systems, governmental/nonprofit accounting
  - **24 semester units in business-related subjects**: business law, economics, finance, management, marketing, statistics, business communications, computer science/information systems, ethics (business or accounting)
  - **20 additional semester units in accounting study**: any combination of accounting subjects (can include graduate-level)
  - **10 semester units in ethics study**: must include accounting ethics (can overlap with business-related units if the course qualifies in both categories)

### Experience Requirements
- **2 years (or 1 year in some cases) of verified general accounting experience**
- Must be under the supervision of a licensed CPA (active, not retired or inactive)
- General accounting experience includes: accounting, attest, compilation, management advisory, financial advisory, tax, consulting
- Supervision: CPA must evaluate and sign off on experience, does not need to be in same physical location but must provide meaningful oversight
- 1-year path: available for candidates with 150+ units who complete experience requirements in a qualifying public accounting firm (verify current CBA rules)

### Ethics Examination
- **Eliminated effective July 1, 2024**
- Previously required: AICPA Professional Ethics Exam (self-study)
- No longer a licensure requirement as of 2024

### Application Process
- Apply to CBA via CBA Connect (online portal)
- Submit transcripts, experience verification, exam scores
- Background check (LiveScan fingerprinting)
- Initial license fee: ~$280 (verify current)
- License issued as "CPA" — no distinction between exam paths once licensed

## 3. California Licensure — AB 1175 Modernized Path

### Legislative History
- **AB 1175** signed by Governor Gavin Newsom on **October 3, 2025**
- **Effective date: January 1, 2027**
- **Transition period: January 1, 2027 – December 31, 2028** (both legacy and modernized paths accepted)
- After December 31, 2028: only the modernized path available for new applicants

### Purpose & Policy Goals
- Remove the rigid 150-unit barrier that was identified as a significant deterrent to CPA licensure
- Increase diversity and access, particularly for underrepresented and first-generation college students
- Align California with emerging national trends to modernize CPA pathways
- Retain educational rigor through specific unit requirements while removing total-unit mandates

### Education Requirements (Modernized Path)
- **Bachelor's degree** from an accredited institution (any major)
- **Accounting concentration** with the following specific units:
  - **24 semester units in accounting subjects** (same qualifying subjects as legacy path)
  - **24 semester units in business-related subjects** (same qualifying subjects as legacy path)
  - **3 semester units in accounting ethics** (more focused than the legacy 10-unit ethics requirement)
- **No 150-semester-unit total requirement** — this is the key change
- A candidate with a 120-unit bachelor's degree + the required accounting and business units qualifies

### Experience Requirements (Modernized Path)
- **2 years of general accounting experience** under CPA supervision (same as legacy)
- **Experience substitution**: advanced degrees (master's, PhD) or CBA-approved certificate programs can substitute for up to **1 year** of the experience requirement
  - Must be in accounting, taxation, or closely related field
  - CBA will publish approved program criteria

### Practical Impact
- A student can now become CPA-eligible with a 4-year bachelor's degree (~120 units) instead of needing a 5th year or master's program
- Estimated to save candidates 1 year of education costs and opportunity cost
- Particularly benefits:
  - Students at CSU and UC campuses who don't want to pursue a master's
  - Career changers who have a bachelor's in another field
  - First-generation and lower-income students for whom the 5th year is a financial barrier

### Key Details for Advisors
- The 150-unit path is NOT eliminated — it remains available during the transition and candidates who already have 150 units are unaffected
- Exam requirements are identical: same Core + Discipline structure
- Once licensed, there is NO distinction between CPAs licensed under legacy vs. modernized path
- CBA is expected to issue detailed regulations and FAQs during 2026

## 4. CPE Requirements & Renewal

### Renewal Cycle
- **Every 2 years** (based on birth month)
- Renewal fee: **$340** (verify current)
- Renewed via **CBA Connect** online portal
- Self-certification of CPE completion — subject to random audit

### CPE Hour Requirements (Per 2-Year Renewal Period)

| Requirement | Hours | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Total CPE** | 80 hours | In the 2-year period immediately preceding license expiration |
| **Minimum per year** | 20 hours | Each of the 2 years |
| **Technical hours per year** | ≥12 hours | Included in the 20/year minimum |
| **Total technical hours** | ≥40 hours | 50% of total must be technical |
| **Ethics** | 4 hours | Every 2-year period |
| **Regulatory Review** | 2 hours | CBA-approved course; required for first renewal and every 6 years thereafter |

### What Counts as "Technical"
- Accounting and auditing
- Taxation
- Management advisory services
- Specialized knowledge (e.g., forensic accounting, valuation, IT audit)
- Government accounting
- Financial planning

### What Counts as "Non-Technical"
- Communication skills, personal development
- Practice management, marketing
- Behavioral ethics (unless classified as regulatory review)
- Computer software (general, non-audit/tax-specific)

### Additional Requirements for Specific Practice Areas

| Practice Area | Additional Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Attest services** (audits, reviews) | Additional A&A CPE (24 hours of A&A in the 2-year period) |
| **Compilation services** | Additional A&A CPE requirements |
| **Preparation engagements** | Additional requirements per SSARS |
| **All of the above** | **4 hours of Fraud CPE** every 2-year period |

### CPE Delivery Methods
- Live in-person (group programs)
- Live webcast/webinar (interactive)
- Self-study (QAS-approved)
- Blended learning
- University/college courses (15 CPE hours per semester unit, 10 per quarter unit)
- Published articles/books (with CBA guidelines)
- Instruction/teaching (preparation + presentation, limited hours)

### Key Rules
- **No carryover**: excess hours from one renewal period do NOT carry to the next
- **Nano learning**: programs less than 50 minutes may qualify in some cases (check current CBA rules)
- **Documentation retention**: keep certificates/records for 5 years from renewal date
- **Providers**: NASBA-registered sponsors, accredited universities, CBA-approved providers

### Renewal Failure / Late Renewal
- Failure to renew by expiration: license goes to **delinquent** status
- Cannot practice or use "CPA" title while delinquent
- Reinstatement: pay delinquent fees + current renewal fee + demonstrate CPE compliance
- Extended delinquency (>5 years): may require re-examination or additional requirements

## 5. CBA Practice Rules & Ethics

### Use of "CPA" Title
- Only active licensees may use "CPA" designation in California
- Applies to all contexts: business cards, letterhead, email signatures, social media, advertising
- Inactive or retired licensees must NOT represent themselves as "CPA" without qualification (e.g., "CPA (Inactive)")

### Firm Registration
- Any firm offering public accountancy services must be registered with CBA
- Includes sole proprietors, partnerships, corporations, LLCs
- Firm name must comply with CBA naming rules (no misleading names)
- At least one owner must be a California-licensed CPA

### Peer Review
- Required for firms performing attest, compilation, or preparation engagements
- Enrollment in an AICPA-approved practice monitoring program
- Peer review cycle: typically every 3 years
- Results reported to CBA

### CBA Disciplinary Process
- Complaints investigated by CBA Enforcement Division
- Grounds: negligence, fraud, conviction of crime, violation of rules, failure to maintain CPE
- Penalties range from citation/fine to license revocation
- All disciplinary actions published on CBA website
- CBA may refer criminal matters to Attorney General

### Key Ethics Standards

**Circular 230 (Federal Practice)**
- §10.21: Knowledge of client's omission — must advise client
- §10.22: Due diligence — must exercise reasonable diligence
- §10.29: Conflicts of interest — may represent conflicting interests with informed written consent
- §10.33: Best practices — communicate clearly, advise on penalties, act fairly and with integrity
- §10.34: Standards for written tax advice — must not base on unreasonable factual/legal assumptions
- §10.35–10.37: Competence, fees, return of client records
- §10.50–10.53: Sanctions framework

**AICPA Code of Professional Conduct**
- Independence (Rule 1.200) — applies to attest engagements
- Integrity and Objectivity (Rule 1.100)
- General Standards (Rule 1.300) — competence, due care, planning, supervision
- Compliance with Standards (Rule 1.310)
- Accounting Principles (Rule 1.320)
- Confidential Client Information (Rule 1.700) — no disclosure without consent (exceptions: legal obligation, peer review, ethics investigation)
- Contingent Fees (Rule 1.510) — prohibited for compilations, audits, original tax returns
- Acts Discreditable (Rule 1.400)

**California Business & Professions Code**
- §5062: Grounds for disciplinary action (dishonesty, negligence, willful violation)
- §5063: Disciplinary proceedings
- §5100: Unprofessional conduct definitions
- §5106: Confidentiality requirements
- §5107: Advertising restrictions (no false/misleading claims)

## 6. Interstate Mobility & Practice Privilege

### Pre-AB 1175 Mobility
- California previously required out-of-state CPAs to obtain a California license or a practice privilege notification to practice in CA
- Substantial equivalency determination was required

### AB 1175 Mobility Modernization (Effective January 1, 2027)
- Out-of-state CPAs from "substantially equivalent" states may practice in California without obtaining a separate CA license or filing a notification
- CBA retains authority to revoke practice privilege for CPAs from states deemed non-equivalent
- If a state's licensure standards are not substantially equivalent to California's, CPAs from that state must still obtain a CA license

### Practical Implications
- Simplifies cross-border practice for multi-state firms
- CBA can act quickly to address quality concerns from non-equivalent states
- California CPAs practicing in other states: check individual state requirements (not all states have adopted similar mobility provisions)
- Mobility does NOT exempt out-of-state CPAs from California tax obligations on CA-source income from their practice
